About Me

Hello, I am David Jahns. (it's pronounced "Johns") I'm one of those hipsters that wears Warby Parkers, tight pants and button-ups, likes craft beers, skateboarding, snowboarding, music, traveling, etc.

Now that we got that out of the way...

I spent the first 20 years of my life growing up in the west suburbs of Chicago. Prior to learning how to code, I used to play and tour in a pop punk band called Farewell Fighter. We were so cool. After (and during) that I had a two year career at Apple in which I became a mobile technician at the Genius Bar. Strangely enough, all of these things played pretty big roles in leading me to my new and current career in UX, web, and mobile development.

I will be graduating from Belmont University in May of 2015 with a BS in Web Development and Programming. I was taught the fundamentals of web design and object oriented programming through coursework that focused on HTML, CSS, Javascript, jQuery, AJAX, PHP, and Java. I love keeping up and continuing to learn all the latest features/technology that come up in the web world. I am constantly doing tutorials on Treehouse to stay on top, and even exploring other things like Swift and iOS Development.

I am currently working/interning at HCA Healthcare on the UX / Creative Design team as a front end developer. In my time there I have been introduced to the Yeoman/Grunt workflow, Git and GitHub, and tons of other vital development tools and technologies that I now can't imagine working without.

Feel free to reach out or check me out on your favorite site. I'm way hotter on Instagram, way funnier on Twitter, and way smarter on GitHub.
