About Me

Hello, I am David Jahns. (it's pronounced "Johns")

I'm one of those hipsters that wears Warby Parkers, tight pants and button-ups, likes craft beers, skateboarding, snowboarding, music, traveling, etc.

Now that we got that out of the way...

I spent the first 20 years of my life growing up in the west suburbs of Chicago. Prior to learning how to code, I used to play and tour in a pop punk band called Farewell Fighter. We were so cool. After that I had a two year career at Apple in which I became a mobile technician at the Genius Bar. Between the constant need for a web presence and t-shirt designs for my band, and my constant exposure to awesome technology at Apple, I decided I wanted to learn how to create websites and apps.

I graduated from Belmont University in May of 2015 with a BS in Web Development and Programming. I was taught the fundamentals of web design and object oriented programming through coursework that focused on HTML, CSS, Javascript, jQuery, AJAX, PHP, and Java. I love keeping up and continuing to learn all the latest features/technology that come up in the web world. I am constantly doing tutorials on Treehouse to stay on top, and even exploring other things like Swift and iOS Development.

I am currently working at HCA Healthcare on the UX / Creative Design team as a front end developer, and freelancing web projects in my free time.

Feel free to reach out via email if you'd like me to work on your site, or check me out on your favorite social network. I'm way hotter on Instagram, way funnier on Twitter, and way smarter on GitHub.
